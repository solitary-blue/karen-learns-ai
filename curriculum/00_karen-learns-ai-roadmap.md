# Your AI Learning Journey

Welcome, Karen! Before we dive in, let's look at the whole map of where we're going together. Think of this as standing at the entrance of a new school and taking a tour before the first day of class.

---

## What We're Building

By the end of this journey, you'll be able to:

- **Talk to AI** and get genuinely useful results
- **Direct AI agents** that take action on your behalf — research, writing, organizing
- **Build a Second Brain** — a personal knowledge system that grows with you and makes your agents smarter
- **Use AI in your classroom** — lesson planning, student materials, and teaching your students about AI

---

## Three Threads

Our curriculum has three threads that weave together, like strands in a braid. You'll see all three throughout your learning.

1. **Agentic AI** — the main story
2. **Your Second Brain** — the knowledge system underneath
3. **The Chat Room** — the place where it all happens

---

## . Thread 1: Agentic AI

Most AI tools today are **chatbots** — you ask, they answer, the conversation ends.

**Agents** are different. They can:
- Read and write files
- Search the web
- Organize your projects
- Do multi-step tasks on their own

Think of it this way: a chatbot is like a **classroom helper** who answers questions. An agent is like a **student teacher** who can actually go do things.

---

## . Thread 2: Your Second Brain

Right now, every time you start a new conversation with AI, it starts with a blank slate. It doesn't remember yesterday.

Your **Second Brain** fixes that. It's a personal notebook (in an app called Obsidian) connected to all your agents. When you learn something, capture an idea, or finish a project — it goes into your Second Brain. Next time you talk to any agent, it already knows your context.

> [!TIP] Why This Matters
> Imagine if every substitute teacher who came to your classroom could instantly read your lesson plans, know your students, and understand your teaching philosophy. That's what the Second Brain does for your AI agents.

---

## . Thread 3: The Chat Room

You'll be talking to your AI agents in a place called **iTerm** — it looks different from what you're used to, but it's really just a chat window with a plain-text interface.

You type. The agent responds. That's it.

> [!NOTE] Don't Worry
> We'll keep the terminal stuff light. If it ever feels like too much, we can build you a friendlier interface. The important thing is the conversation, not the window it happens in.

---

## The Journey: 8 Sections

Here's the map. We'll go through it section by section, with 3–6 lessons in each.

---

## Sections 1 & 2: Foundations

**Meeting Your AI** — What AI is, how to talk to it, how it remembers (and forgets), and how to spot when it's making things up.

**Communicating Effectively** — Getting from "okay" answers to genuinely useful ones. Giving context, iterating, using personas, and knowing good output from bad.

> [!SUCCESS] You've Already Started
> We've done some of this together! Your Git Scrapbook and LLM Memory lessons are part of Section 1.

---

## Section 3: From Chatbot to Agent

This is where things get interesting. You'll learn:

- What makes agents different from chatbots
- The **Think-Do-Check Cycle** — how agents work through problems
- What tools agents have and what they can actually do
- How to read what your agent is doing (and when to step in)

---

## Section 4: Your Agent Workspace

Your agent works inside a **workspace** — the folders, files, and instructions that shape what it knows and how it behaves.

This section is about understanding that environment:
- How files and folders are organized
- How instructions (like classroom rules) guide your agent
- How skills give your agent specialized knowledge
- How sessions work — starting fresh vs. picking up where you left off

---

## Section 5: Building Your Second Brain

This is where you set up your personal knowledge system:

- **Obsidian** — a notebook app where your knowledge lives
- **Slack capture** — a quick way to jot down thoughts from anywhere
- **Agent access** — connecting your agents to your notes so they know your context
- **The Daily Review** — a short ritual where you decide what's worth keeping

---

## Sections 6 & 7: Putting It All Together

**Agent Workflows** — Using agents for real work you care about: research, writing, lesson planning, and organizing your knowledge.

**AI in the Classroom** — Applying everything to your teaching: creating lesson plans, building student materials, teaching your students about AI, and thinking through ethics.

> [!TIP] This Is the Payoff
> Everything before this builds toward these sections. By the time you get here, you'll have agents that know your context, and you'll know how to direct them.

---

## Section 8: Growing Your Toolkit

The final section is about **independence**:

- Evaluating different AI tools and choosing the right one
- Customizing your agent's instructions and skills
- Planning where you go next

The goal is that you don't need a guide anymore — you can explore on your own.

---

## How Long Will This Take?

There are 38 lessons total. Each one is a short slideshow like this one, with a hands-on activity.

We won't rush. Some weeks we'll do several lessons, some weeks we'll spend time on a project instead. The pace follows you.

> [!NOTE] The Montessori Way
> Just like in your classroom — we follow the learner. If something clicks fast, we move on. If something needs more time, we take it.
